### 1. The Science of the Hook
The hook is everything before the "see more" button. It must establish credibility and stop the scroll [1].
*   **The 8-Word Rule:** Keep your first two lines around eight words each so they don't cut off on mobile devices.
*   **Character Limits:** Aim for 65 characters on the first line and 45 on the second [3, 4].
*   **Pattern Interrupts:** Use specific, "bizarre" numbers (e.g., "318,842 posts" instead of "300k+") to grab attention.
*   **Example:** *"I built two six-figure SAS products using Vive coding. You don't need clean code to hit six figures"*.

### 2. Proven Writing Frameworks
Once they click "see more," you need to retain them with clear, skimmable value.
*   **The S.L.A.Y. Framework:** 
    *   **S**tory: Start with a personal hook or moment.
    *   **L**esson: Provide the core insight.
    *   **A**ctionable Advice: Give tactical steps.
    *   **Y**ou: Point it back to the reader or ask a question.
*   **P.A.S. (Problem, Agitate, Solution):** Identify a pain point, explain why it’s costing them (money/time), and provide your unique fix.

### 3. Formatting for "Dwell Time"
LinkedIn rewards posts that keep users on the page.
*   **White Space:** Use short sentences (1–3 lines max) and avoid large "wall of text" paragraphs [9, 10].
*   **Visual Authority:** Carousels and cheat sheets perform 2x better for impressions and follower conversion than text alone.
*   **Human Cues:** Posts with unpolished, real photos of you often outperform sterile, AI-generated graphics because they build trust [12, 13].

### 4. Conversion and Call to Action (CTA)
Don’t leave growth to chance; tell the user exactly what to do.
*   **The Soft Reminder:** Simply asking people to "follow for more" is the easiest way to convert a reader into a follower.
*   **Engagement Questions:** End with a specific question like, *"Which framework will you implement today?"* to spark comments.
*   **Lead Magnets:** Offer a free resource (a "lead magnet") in exchange for a comment. This creates a "viral mechanic" where high engagement pushes the post to more people [16, 17].

**Example of a High-Value Post Structure:**
1. **Hook:** *"Most engineers will never build a profitable product. I was one of them for years"*.
2. **Expansion:** Briefly explain the struggle of over-engineering.
3. **The Value:** A list of 5 steps to build a "Vibe Coded" MVP in a weekend.
4. **CTA:** *"I’m giving away the full SOP for this. Comment 'VIBE' and I’ll DM it to you"*
